About MetroHacks Women

This year marks MetroHacksâ€™ third annual hackathon for female students, providing completely free and open opportunities for girls aged 13-18 regardless of experience levels.

In light of the recent pandemic, the third annual MetroHacks Hackathon will transition to a virtual event. Solving real-world problems is the purpose of hackathons. Amidst the chaos and uncertainties cast by the COVID-19 pandemic, we aim to bring the MetroHacks community together to identify problems that have emerged in this crisis and take the initiative to solve them using innovation, technology, and compassion. Through this effort, MetroHacks is also devoted to bringing more female students into the STEM fields.

Students will acquire a deeper understanding of the COVID-19 pandemic, learn about various technologies through several webinar workshops, collaborate and create projects with team members across the country, and compete for prizes. The event is completely free and open to all high school girls regardless of expertise!

Tracks

The pandemic poses challenges in virtually every aspect of our lives, from distance learning to quarantine and social isolation. It would be helpful, therefore, to identify a field you are passionate to work in and dedicate your energy to resolving specific problems within that area. Here are the three that we suggest:

Medical

Hack a project that directly aids the COVID-19 patients, their families, or those working tirelessly in the healthcare industry to look after us all! This can cover patient monitoring outside a clinic, early diagnosis, effective techniques to prevent infection, lower the infection risk for healthcare workers, and many more possibilities! Projects in this track generally ensure the physical well-being of users or help medical professionals in some way.

Social

Devise a project that leverages technology to improve our community, government or society in this difficult time. Develop an app or code up a website - use your creativity and talents to urge people to take civic responsibility into their own hands. This can be finding more creative ways to urge people to strictly follow social distancing guidelines and wash their hands, helping isolated individuals or households to stay connected with each other, exploring safer ways to get groceries, even aiding victims of domestic violence since we are required to be home more now.

Education

While distance learning might mean you can stay in bed for your morning classes, it can be a great challenge for many. Use your hacking skills to improve the accessibility and quality of education! Keep in mind that education can mean more than formal schooling: obtaining truthful, timely, and easily understandable information about COVID-19 and government policies, learning how to best sterilize yourself and belongings after going to crowded places, or skills that are not taught in schools that can be learned at home like budgeting, etc. - these are all necessary types of education for us right now. You can reflect on the past weeks of online learning and try to make it better; devise products for those without constant internet access to keep learning and stay informed; connect information across the globe to provide more wholistic reports on the virus, etc. Be creative! :)

FAQ

Who can come?

Young Women, aged 13-18, can come! No experience required - if you're motivated and love tech, you're welcome at MetroHacks Women.

How much does it cost?

MetroHacks Women costs nothing. Registering and attending is free, and you get free food, t-shirts, prizes, swag and lessons.

What should I bring?

Bring a laptop, charger, motivation, and great ideas! We'll take care of the rest.

When is it?

April 24th, 2020

Where is it?

This event is being run virtually.

How do I form a team?

You'll have time to find a team when you arrive. If you already have a team, just list your teammates when you register. You may work individually or in teams of no more than 4 people.

What time does the event end?

This hackathon is a two day event starting on April 24, 2020 and ending on April 25, 2020 @ 7:00 PM
